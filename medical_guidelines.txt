Hypertension Management Protocol (Protocol 14.1-IM): First-line treatments per JNC/ACC-AHA guidelines include Thiazide diuretics, ACE inhibitors, ARBs, or Calcium Channel Blockers. Target BP is <130/80 mmHg for most adults. Lifestyle modifications such as the DASH diet, sodium restriction, weight management, regular exercise, and alcohol moderation are strictly recommended.

Type 2 Diabetes Mellitus Protocol (Protocol 3.8-ENDO): Management involves Metformin as the initial pharmacologic agent along with comprehensive lifestyle modifications. If A1C target is not achieved after 3 months, consider adding a SGLT2 inhibitor (especially if established ASCVD or heart failure), a GLP-1 receptor agonist, or a DPP-4 inhibitor. Insulin therapy should be considered if A1C >10% or if patient is symptomatic.

Asthma Exacerbation Protocol (Protocol 7.2-RESP): Management follows a stepwise approach. Step 1: SABA as needed. Step 2: Low-dose ICS. Step 3: Low-dose ICS/LABA. Step 4: Medium-dose ICS/LABA. Step 5: High-dose ICS/LABA plus add-on therapy (e.g., tiotropium, biologics). For acute exacerbations: administer Salbutamol nebulizer, systemic corticosteroids, and supplemental O2.

Toxicology Opioid Overdose Protocol (Protocol 4.2-TOX): Standard protocol requires verifying patient weight and immediately administering Naloxone (0.4-2.0mg IV/IM/SC) for suspected overdose. Monitor the respiratory rate and maintain the airway. Re-dose every 2-3 minutes if there is no response, up to a maximum of 10mg.

Cardiology Acute MI Protocol (Protocol 5.1-CARD): Management of acute myocardial infarction requires immediate administration of Aspirin (162-325mg chewed), a 12-lead ECG monitor, and urgent evaluation for reperfusion therapy (PCI or Fibrinolytics) within 90 minutes of medical contact. Administer Nitroglycerin sublingually for chest pain unless contraindicated.

Neurology Ischemic Stroke Protocol (Protocol 9.1-NEURO): Acute ischemic stroke management requires a CT head to definitively exclude hemorrhage, followed by IV Alteplase (0.9mg/kg, max 90mg) within a restrictive 4.5hr window. Consider mechanical thrombectomy for large vessel occlusion within 24 hours. Monitor BP tightly, manage glucose, and control temperature. Start antiplatelet therapy after 24hr.
